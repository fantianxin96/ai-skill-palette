Turn a project or AI collaboration session into reusable memory, learning, and shareable reflection.

## When To Use

Use this when the user wants to summarize a project, preserve hard-won debugging/context for future AI chats, understand their own design decisions, write an Obsidian note, or draft a social media reflection.

## Workflow

1. **Identify recap type**
   - AI collaboration memory: what future chats need to know.
   - Design process recap: how the design evolved and why.
   - Product/project retrospective: what changed, what worked, what remains.
   - Public post: a polished, readable story for social sharing.

2. **Gather evidence**
   - Inspect relevant files, commits, changelog entries, screenshots, messages, or notes when available.
   - Separate facts from interpretation.

3. **Extract the useful learning**
   - What was the original problem?
   - What attempts failed or felt wrong?
   - What decision unlocked progress?
   - What pattern should be reused next time?
   - What warning should future AI know?

4. **Write for the destination**
   - Obsidian memory: structured, concise, searchable.
   - New chat context: short brief with key files, decisions, and pitfalls.
   - Design learning: reflective and specific.
   - Social post: narrative, human, not too technical unless desired.

## Output Format

Choose the format that fits the user's goal:

- Project recap
- AI collaboration memory
- Design decision log
- Next-chat context brief
- Social post draft

## Quality Bar

A good recap makes the next round better. It should help the user remember what they learned, help future AI avoid repeating solved mistakes, and preserve the reasoning behind design decisions.

Never turn a recap into generic praise. Capture concrete decisions, causes, tradeoffs, and next actions.
